#+title:    Auto-populating the Staples Tracker
#+language: en

* What this documents

The Staples Tracker's "Auto" button offers a menu of longevity diets and fills
the tracker with that diet's essential staples.  This records how the diets and
their staples were chosen, so the lists can be revisited with the reasoning in
hand rather than reverse-engineered from code.

* Choosing the diets

** The shortlist and the verdicts

| Diet              | Verdict  | Reasoning                                                                                     |
|-------------------+----------+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Mediterranean     | Included | The most-studied longevity pattern (PREDIMED and decades of cohort data); already an app tag. |
| Blue Zone         | Included | The explicit longevity brand — the shared pattern of the five long-lived regions.             |
| MIND              | Included | Mediterranean-DASH hybrid built for brain aging, with named food-group targets.               |
| DASH              | Included | Strong outcome evidence via blood pressure; a pillar of the app's recipe collection.          |
| Anti-Inflammatory | Included | Chronic inflammation is the through-line of aging; the app tags recipes with it already.      |
| Okinawan          | Folded   | One of the five Blue Zones; its staples (sweet potato, tofu, green tea) enter through there.  |
| Nordic            | Omitted  | Genuinely healthful, but its evidence base and food list largely restate Mediterranean.       |
| Ornish / vegan    | Omitted  | A therapeutic reversal protocol more than a longevity pattern; poor fit for a staples list.   |
| Keto / paleo      | Omitted  | Popular, but without longevity evidence; contradicts the pyramid's legume-and-grain base.     |

** The deciding constraint: consistency with the rest of the app

The recipe collection is already tagged =Mediterranean=, =Blue Zone=, =MIND=,
=DASH=, and =Anti-Inflammatory=, and the About page names the same five
approaches.  Offering exactly these five means a user can auto-stock staples for
a diet and then filter recipes by the same name — the feature teaches the app's
own vocabulary instead of introducing a parallel one.  The menu uses the tags'
exact spellings for the same reason.

* Choosing the staples

** Ground rules

1. /Staples, not groceries./ Each list is the pantry backbone a follower of that
   diet reaches for weekly — not produce variety, which the pyramid itself
   teaches.
2. /Keeps, or it is not a staple./ Staples favour dry goods, jars, cans, the
   freezer, and the spice cupboard.  Leafy greens are excluded outright — they
   are weekly fresh shopping, not stock — and vegetables make the list only when
   they keep for weeks (alliums, carrots, cabbage, winter roots).  Perishable
   forms give way to pantry forms: tomato paste over fresh tomatoes, canned
   salmon over fresh.  Berries stay because the freezer stocks them as well as
   the produce aisle does.
3. /Every name is a catalog name./ A tracker chip only gets its tier tint, stock
   tracking, and shopping-list identity when its name matches a Longevity Foods
   entry exactly, so every staple is spelled precisely as the bundled catalog
   spells it ("Banana", "Sweet potato", "Extra-virgin olive oil").  A
   verification pass checks every staple against the seed, and that none sits in
   the catalog's leafy-greens category.
4. /Sixteen per diet./ Enough to be a real starter pantry, small enough to read
   at a glance and prune by hand.
5. /Distinctive over generic./ Where diets overlap (they all like beans), each
   list leans into what distinguishes the diet — corn tortillas and miso for
   Blue Zone, berries and poultry for MIND, low-fat dairy and potassium fruit
   for DASH, the spice rack for Anti-Inflammatory.

** The lists and their reasoning

*** Mediterranean

Olive oil as the principal fat; legumes and intact grains as the base; the
jarred and canned Mediterranean larder (tomato paste, olives, capers) with
long-keeping alliums; nuts, fermented dairy, and small oily fish as the
signature proteins.

Extra-virgin olive oil, Chickpeas, Lentils, Farro, Whole-wheat pasta, Tomato
paste, Garlic, Onions, Capers, Lemons, Olives, Almonds, Walnuts, Greek yogurt,
Feta, Canned sardines.

*** Blue Zone

The common denominators of the five regions: beans at every meal, slow
carbohydrates, long-keeping garden vegetables — plus each region's signature
(Okinawa's sweet potato, tofu, edamame, and green tea; Nicoya's corn tortillas;
Sardinia and Ikaria's olive oil; Loma Linda's nuts and oats).

Black beans, Chickpeas, Fava beans, Brown rice, Oats, Whole-grain corn
tortillas, Sweet potato, Cabbage, Edamame, Garlic, Onions, Tofu, Miso, Walnuts,
Extra-virgin olive oil, Green tea.

*** MIND

The MIND trial scores ten food groups; the shelf-stable ones map to staples
directly: berries (blueberries and strawberries are the studied ones, stocked
frozen), whole grains, beans, nuts, olive oil, fish from the can, and poultry
from the freezer.  Its daily leafy greens are deliberately absent — that is
fresh shopping, not stock.

Blueberries, Strawberries, Chickpeas, Peanut butter, Whole-wheat pasta, Oats,
Quinoa, Whole-grain bread, Brown rice, Black beans, Lentils, Walnuts, Almonds,
Extra-virgin olive oil, Canned salmon, Chicken.

*** DASH

Built around potassium, magnesium, calcium, and fibre: fruit and long-keeping
vegetables in volume, whole grains, legumes, low-fat dairy, lean protein,
unsalted nuts.

Oats, Brown rice, Whole-grain bread, Black beans, Kidney beans, Lentils, Banana,
Oranges, Apples, Onions, Broccoli, Carrots, Sweet potato, Greek yogurt, Almonds,
Chicken.

*** Anti-Inflammatory

The spice rack does the heavy lifting (turmeric, ginger, cinnamon, garlic, and
the black pepper that unlocks turmeric's curcumin), with omega-3 fish, frozen
polyphenol berries, freezer-friendly crucifers, storage alliums, omega-3 seeds,
and green tea rounding it out.

Turmeric, Ginger, Cinnamon, Garlic, Black pepper, Extra-virgin olive oil,
Salmon, Canned sardines, Blueberries, Tart cherries, Onions, Broccoli, Walnuts,
Flaxseed, Chia seeds, Green tea.

* Behaviour decisions

- /No duplicates./ A staple already in the tracker (case-insensitively) is
  skipped, so re-running a diet — or stacking two overlapping diets — never
  doubles a chip.  Stacking is deliberate: the lists are additive, and a user
  following "Mediterranean plus MIND" gets the union.
- /Nothing is removed./ Auto-populate only adds; hand-curated staples are never
  touched.  Clearing is what chip ✕ buttons are for.
- /The menu lives under the pane's title bar/ and only shows while the tracker
  is expanded, like the rest of the pane's contents.
