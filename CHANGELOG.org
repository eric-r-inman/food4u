#+title:     Changelog
#+language:  en

* Upcoming
** Added
- Bookmark a recipe from its header, between the drag grip and the cart button —
  a marker for remembering a recipe, such as one whose ingredients you have just
  sent to the Shopping List. A recipe category's title row shows how many of its
  recipes are bookmarked.
- Select several items and move them together: a "Select: off / on" toggle in
  its own row under the page title turns on a tap-to-select circle on every item
  badge. Move the whole selection either by dragging one selected badge, or —
  with no dragging at all, so it works on a phone — by pressing the green ⬇
  "Move selected here" arrow that appears on each Kitchen pane, Longevity
  category, and Shopping List category while items are selected; the items stay
  selected so you can send them somewhere else too. A "Deselect all" button
  appears beside the toggle once more than one item is picked. Badges still drag
  individually with a click-and-hold.
- Organize the Shopping List into your own categories: it starts with a default
  set (Produce, Canned & Dry Goods, Dairy, and so on), and you can add your own,
  drag to-buy items into any of them, and delete a category — with a
  confirmation — from the ✕ on its title row.  Each category collapses to a
  labelled row with a count, matching the Recipes column.  Recipe and staple
  additions still land in the main list, ready to sort.  (The automatic
  grocery-department grouping is replaced by these categories.)
- Delete a Longevity Foods category with the ✕ on its title row, with a
  confirmation.
- Add your own food category to any tier of the Longevity Foods pyramid, then
  fill it with foods like the built-in categories.
- Generate recipes with AI: an "AI" button in each recipe category opens an
  assistant that creates a recipe from the meal category, your remembered
  preferences and allergies, and — if you like — what is in your Kitchen; you
  preview it before it is added, and its missing ingredients can go straight to
  the Shopping List.  You bring your own LLM key (Google Gemini's free tier,
  OpenAI/ChatGPT, or Anthropic Claude); the request goes from your browser
  directly to the provider and your key is kept only in your browser.
- Sign in and sign out through an OIDC identity provider, with each account
  keeping its own kitchen.
- Add and remove Kitchen storage panes.
- Edit a Kitchen pane's name and description inline — a pencil opens the editor,
  Enter saves, and Escape cancels without saving.
- A Staples Tracker pane in the Kitchen: drag in the foods you always want on
  hand, see the ones you are out of turn red, and send those to the Shopping
  List with one button.
- Move a recipe to another category by dragging it there; the recipe's own
  category collapses as you start, and categories highlight as you drag over
  them.
- Seed a new account with the default storage panes on its first sign-in.
- =just login-demo= runs the app against a local identity provider for trying
  the signed-in experience.
- Rename the app to Longevity Pantry (the code keeps the =food4u= name).
- Recolour a Kitchen pane's header from the app's palette while editing it, and
  delete a pane — with a confirmation — from the same editor.
** Fixes
- A recipe's cart button is now green only when the recipe has an ingredient
  that is neither in the Kitchen nor already on the Shopping List; when there is
  nothing left to add it is greyed out, so a glance says which recipes still
  need shopping.
- The "Paste" and "AI" buttons in a recipe category now work: pressing one no
  longer dismisses the add row before the click takes effect.
- Adding items after a fresh page load — by dragging, by the Staples Tracker
  cart button, and so on — no longer occasionally fails to save; newly created
  items are given ids that cannot collide with existing ones.
- The Staples Tracker now starts collapsed, and its shopping-cart button stands
  out clearly against the header — and turns green when it holds staples not yet
  in the Kitchen or on the Shopping List.
- Editing a Kitchen pane no longer clips the controls on the right edge, and
  clicking away from the pane closes the editor without saving.
