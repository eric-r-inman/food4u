Longevity Pantry

"Not today, preventable diseases!"

Longevity Pantry is a foods-first kitchen companion, tracking longevity foods in your pantry and fridge, and using those foods to build recipes and shopping lists that support Mediterranean, Blue Zone, MIND, anti-inflammatory, and DASH approaches to healthful eating. Features require sign-in.

How it works

I developed Longevity Pantry because I wanted a kitchen companion that focused on longevity foods first, making it easy to know which target foods I had on hand, and which ones I needed to shop for. Then, using my target foods as the building blocks, I wanted to easily create recipes, meal plans, and shopping lists. This app is the result, and I use it every darned day. Sign in to try it out, and if you like it as much as I do and you plan to use it as your own kitchen companion, please consider donating so I can continue to host and update the app.

  • Longevity Foods — 500–plus foods arranged by how often to eat them, based on science-backed, longevity-focused dietary guidelines. Add, move, or remove foods to make the list your own.
  • Recipes — Stocked with recipes inspired by longevity diets, organized by dish type, and filterable by tags. Curate the list to your liking, and to add or paste in your own recipes.
  • Meal Planner — Plan your meals for the week by dragging recipe cards into breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snack slots.
  • Kitchen — Track longevity foods in your pantry, refrigerator, freezer, and spice cupboard. A staples tracker lets you keep tabs on the foods you always want to have on hand.
  • Shopping List — One-click add out-of-stock staples or ingredients for recipes. Or, drag-and-drop foods from the Longevity Foods list. One-click export a text file shopping list.

The philosophy

It's too easy to over-manage and over-think a kitchen. Start with science-backed longevity foods, and let those be our guide for stocking the pantry and fridge, recipe curation, meal planning, and food shopping.

Your data

When you're signed in, your kitchen is unique, and your changes are saved to the server. If you use the AI recipe assistant, you bring your own key, and requests go straight from your browser to your chosen provider; the key never touches this server.
