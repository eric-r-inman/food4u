Longevity Pantry

Eat for the long run

Longevity Pantry is an ingredients-first kitchen companion, tracking longevity foods in your pantry and refrigerator. Easily build recipes and shopping lists that support Mediterranean, Blue Zone, MIND, anti-inflammatory, and DASH approaches to healthful eating.

How it works

The app is a row of columns, each collapsible to a slim bar, each one step in the same loop: know your foods, keep them stocked, cook from them, and plan the days ahead.

  • Longevity Foods — a pyramid of five hundred–plus foods arranged by how often to eat them, from every-meal foundations (greens, olive oil, whole grains) to once-in-a-while extras. Add your own foods and categories, and mark what is in stock as your kitchen changes.
  • Recipes — a recipe box stocked with dishes from the longevity canons, organized by dish type and filterable by tag: diet plan, meal, convenience (quick, one-pot, no-cook, Instant Pot, slow cooker), or featured protein. Each recipe knows its ingredients, shows what you can cook right now from what is on hand, and sends whatever is missing to the shopping list in one press. Add your own recipes by typing, pasting, or asking the built-in AI assistant.
  • Meal Planner — lay the days ahead out by dragging recipe cards into breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snack slots across as many days as you plan for. The same recipe can be planned as often as you like, and every planned card jumps back to its recipe.
  • Kitchen — your real storage, pane by pane: pantry, refrigerator, freezer, spice cupboard, and any pane you add. A staples tracker watches the foods you always want on hand and flags the ones you have run out of.
  • Shopping List — everything the other columns decided you need, sorted into your own categories, exportable as plain text for the trip to the store.

The philosophy

No calorie counting, no streaks, no guilt. The premise is gentler and older: surround yourself with good ingredients, keep them where you can see them, and let the default choice be the healthy one. The pyramid says what to keep close; the planner and shopping list just shorten the distance between intention and dinner.

Your data

Your kitchen is yours. Everything you add — foods, recipes, plans, lists — lives in your own account's kitchen. If you use the AI recipe assistant, you bring your own key, and requests go straight from your browser to your chosen provider; the key never touches this server.
